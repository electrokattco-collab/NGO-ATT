Our Flagship Initiative

Supporting Resilience
Mental Wellness.

An innovative, long-term (8+ years) school-based program providing comprehensive mental wellness interventions for learners aged 6-18 in under-resourced communities across South Africa.

Program Overview

A Multi-Tiered Approach to Mental Wellness.

The Supporting Resilience Mental Wellness (SRMW) initiative employs a comprehensive, multi-tiered approach to mental health support in schools. By embedding mental wellness support directly within school environments and engaging the entire ecosystem of educators, parents, and communities, we create lasting change.

Universal Screening

Classroom observations and early identification of at-risk learners

Targeted Interventions

Individual and group counselling for identified learners

Intensive Support

Specialized care for high-need cases with external referrals

Ecosystem Engagement

Educator training and parental support services

Our Methodology

The SRMW Counselling Model

01

Identification Phase

Classroom-based behavioural observations, teacher and parent consultations, screening assessments, and initial rapport building.

02

Classification Phase

Comprehensive needs assessment, risk evaluation, diagnostic impressions, and individualized treatment planning.

03

Intervention Phase

Evidence-based therapeutic approaches (CBT, trauma-focused CBT, play therapy, narrative therapy), individual and group sessions.

04

Monitoring Phase

Regular progress reviews, outcome measurement, treatment plan adjustments, and stakeholder feedback integration.

05

Transition Phase

Relapse prevention planning, skill consolidation, referral to ongoing support if needed, and follow-up check-ins.

Who We Serve

Target Beneficiaries

Our SRMW initiative serves learners, families, educators, and entire school communities in under-resourced areas across South Africa.

Primary: Learners

Children and adolescents aged 6-18 years (Grade R through Grade 12) attending schools in under-resourced communities, particularly those experiencing trauma, poverty-related stress, family dysfunction, behavioural challenges, or academic difficulties linked to social-emotional factors.

Secondary: Parents

Families of identified learners who receive parental counselling, guidance on supporting children's emotional needs, resources and referrals to community services, and workshops on positive parenting and family communication.

Secondary: Educators

Teachers and school staff who benefit from training on trauma-informed practices, emotional support and debriefing services, consultation on managing challenging behaviours, and resources for creating supportive classroom environments.
